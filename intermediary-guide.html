Section 2: Advanced Nutrition Strategies

Strategic Fueling

  • Training Days: Higher carbs, timed protein, strategic fats
  • Recovery Days: Moderate carbs, anti-inflammatory fats, hydration focus
  • Rest Days: Lower carbs, higher fats, gut support

Macro Targets

  • Protein: 0.8–1.0g per lb bodyweight
  • Carbs: Adjust based on training intensity
  • Fats: Prioritize omega-3s, MCTs, and monounsaturated sources

Meal Timing

  • Pre-Workout: Protein + carbs (e.g., chicken + rice)
  • Post-Workout: Protein + fast-digesting carbs (e.g., whey + banana)
  • Evening: Protein + fats + fiber (e.g., salmon + greens)

Anti-Inflammatory Additions

  • Turmeric + black pepper
  • Ginger, garlic, cinnamon
  • Leafy greens, berries, cruciferous vegetables

Quick Wins

  • Use protein at every meal
  • Time carbs around training
  • Use herbs and spices for anti-inflammatory support
  • Hydrate early and often

Nutrition isn’t just fuel—it’s feedback. Use it to support performance, recovery, and long-term resilience. Track your response, adjust your strategy, and stay consistent.

Section 3: Progressive Training & Recovery

Training Splits

  • 3-Day Split: Push / Pull / Legs
  • 4-Day Split: Upper / Lower / Conditioning / Recovery
  • 5-Day Split: Strength / Hypertrophy / Conditioning / Mobility / Recovery

Recovery Protocols

  • Use active recovery (walking, mobility)
  • Prioritize sleep and hydration
  • Track HRV or RHR if possible
  • Use magnesium and glycine before bed
  • Use breathwork post-training to reset

Training Intensity

  • RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion): Use 1–10 scale to guide effort
  • Progressive Overload: Increase reps, sets, or load weekly
  • Deload Weeks: Every 4–6 weeks, reduce volume to recover

Quick Wins

  • Train with intention, not just volume
  • Recover like it’s part of the program
  • Use breathwork and mobility daily
  • Track performance and adjust weekly

Training creates the stimulus. Recovery builds the result. Balance both to unlock sustainable progress. Your body adapts when you give it the right challenge—and the right support.

Section 4: Circadian Health & Stress Modulation

Why Circadian Health Matters

Your biology runs on rhythm. When your light exposure, sleep timing, and stress inputs align with your circadian clock, everything improves—energy, recovery, hormones, and mood.

Morning Protocol

  • Get sunlight within 30 minutes of waking
  • Hydrate with electrolytes
  • Delay caffeine 60–90 minutes
  • Use movement to signal wakefulness

Evening Protocol

  • Dim lights 2 hours before bed
  • Avoid screens or use blue light filters
  • Use magnesium and glycine to support sleep
  • Use breathwork or NSDR to downshift

Stress Modulation Tools

  • Box breathing or 4-7-8 technique
  • NSDR (Non-Sleep Deep Rest) audio sessions
  • Adaptogens like ashwagandha or rhodiola
  • Journaling and gratitude practice

Stress isn’t the enemy—poor recovery is. Use these tools to stay resilient, calm, and focused.

Section 5: Supplement Optimization

Why Optimization Matters

Supplements aren’t shortcuts—they’re strategic tools. At the beginner level, you built a foundation. Now it’s time to refine your stack, dial in timing, and support performance, recovery, and hormonal balance with precision.

Core Stack (Still Essential)

  • Magnesium Glycinate: 200–400mg before bed. Supports sleep, recovery, and stress modulation.
  • Omega-3 (EPA/DHA): 1,000–2,000mg daily. Reduces inflammation, supports brain and heart health.
  • Vitamin D3 + K2: 2,000–5,000 IU daily. Supports immunity, mood, and calcium metabolism.
  • Electrolytes: Sodium, potassium, magnesium. Use in morning water or post-training.
  • Protein Powder: 1–2 scoops daily. Use post-workout or in meals to hit protein targets.

Performance Add-Ons

  • Creatine Monohydrate: 3–5g daily. Supports strength, cognition, and lean mass.
  • L-Citrulline: 6–8g pre-workout. Boosts nitric oxide and blood flow.
  • Beta-Alanine: 2–5g daily. Buffers lactic acid, improves endurance.
  • Electrolyte Mix: Use during long or intense training sessions.

Recovery & Resilience

  • Ashwagandha: 300–600mg daily. Adaptogen that lowers cortisol and supports mood.
  • Rhodiola Rosea: 200–400mg in the morning. Supports energy and stress resilience.
  • Glycine: 3–5g before bed. Supports sleep depth and collagen synthesis.
  • Collagen Peptides: 10g daily. Supports joints, skin, and connective tissue.

Gut & Hormonal Support

  • Probiotic: Multi-strain, refrigerated if possible. Supports digestion and immunity.
  • Digestive Enzymes: Use with larger meals. Supports nutrient absorption.
  • Zinc + Selenium: Supports thyroid, testosterone, and immune function.
  • Adaptogen Blends: Rotate monthly to avoid tolerance.

Timing & Cycling

  • Morning: Vitamin D3/K2, Omega-3, Rhodiola, Electrolytes
  • Pre-Workout: Creatine, Citrulline, Beta-Alanine
  • Post-Workout: Protein, Collagen, Electrolytes
  • Evening: Magnesium, Ashwagandha, Glycine
  • Cycling: Rotate adaptogens monthly. Take 1–2 weeks off every 8–12 weeks.

Quick Wins

  • Creatine daily = strength + cognition
  • Magnesium + glycine = deeper sleep
  • Electrolytes = better hydration and energy
  • Adaptogens = calmer mood and better resilience

Supplements support your strategy—not replace it. Use them with intention, track your response, and upgrade your recovery, performance, and resilience.

Section 6: Mindset Architecture

Why Architecture Matters

Mindset isn’t motivation—it’s infrastructure. It’s the system that supports your habits, identity, and resilience. This section helps you build mental tools that hold up under stress, distraction, and doubt. You’re not just reacting—you’re designing your psychology.

Identity Reinforcement

  • “I am someone who trains with purpose.”
  • “I fuel my body with intention.”
  • “I recover like an athlete.”
  • “I protect my energy and focus.”

Repeat these daily. Write them. Speak them. Live them. Identity drives behavior.

Habit Psychology

  • Trigger → Action → Reward: Anchor new habits to existing ones
  • Environment Design: Remove friction. Make good choices easy and visible
  • Micro Wins: Celebrate small actions. Momentum compounds
  • Self-Compassion: Progress isn’t linear. Reframe setbacks as data, not failure

Resilience Tools

  • NSDR (Non-Sleep Deep Rest): Use guided audio to reset your nervous system
  • Breathwork: Box breathing, 4-7-8, or physiological sigh to calm stress
  • Visualization: Picture your future self acting with strength and clarity
  • Journaling: Track wins, gratitudes, and intentions nightly

Focus Rituals

  • Start day with intention: “Today I choose progress over perfection”
  • Use Pomodoro or time-blocking for deep work
  • Protect mornings from reactive tasks (email, social media)
  • End day with reflection and reset

Affirmations That Stick

  • “I am proud of my effort.”
  • “I am building momentum every day.”
  • “I fuel my body with purpose.”
